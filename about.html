About

Welcome to Pictures & Words

This website is my personal display shelf, where I can stash my collections of quotes from some of my favorite books, as well as catalogue my photography.

Why I Like Photography

The tragedy of experiencing something beautiful is that it's going to die. That is intentionally dramatic phrasing, but photography, while not immortalizing moments, at least helps them to last. It serves as a tangible touchstone to bring you back to a place that would otherwise only exist in your memory. Photos become launching points for storiesâ€”a way to share an experience with those who weren't there. And that's nice.

Why I Like Books

Books take a long time to write, especially good ones. In a few pages, you can read something that may have taken the author 20 years to understand. Just as photos capture moments, books capture ideas. Reading classics like the Iliad or Greek tragedies provides a common ground between myself and everyone who has read or heard those stories. I often imagine an afterlife where I can discuss favorite passages from the Iliad or Odyssey with someone who lived thousands of years ago. Besides the connection to the past, books help me to work through my own beliefs. The fact that written arguments defending the beliefs of people from the past exist means people can understand and carry that conversation forward into the future. I'm currently reconsidering my Protestant worldview, and instead of having to work out everything on my own, I can read the words of Martin Luther and have him explain to me the necessity of breaking from the Catholic Church, or go back further and read the Church Fathers having them explain to me why the Orthodox Church today stands apart from Catholicism.
