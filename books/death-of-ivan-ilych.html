The Death of Ivan Ilych by Leo Tolstoy

Published: 1886

A brief precise depiction of the life and death of one man. An honest and precise examination of life that reveals that most of what our lifes work consists of is are socially acceptable distractions from the death that awaits you at this moment. An easy read in one sitting that will sit with you forever.

Notable Quotes

  • "Maybe I did not live as I ought to have done?" is what suddenly came into his head. "But how could that be, when I did everything properly?" - Leo Tolstoy
  • "The example of a syllogism that he had studied in Kiesewetter's logic: Caius is a man, men are mortal, therefore Caius is mortal, had throughout his whole life seemed to him right only in relation to Caius, but not to him at all." - Leo Tolstoy
  • "It is as if I had been going downhill while I imagined I was going up. And that is really what it was. I was going up in public opinion, but to the same extent life was ebbing away from me." - Leo Tolstoy
  • "Ivan Ilyich's life had been most simple and most ordinary and therefore most terrible." - Leo Tolstoy
  • "The deception, the lie, which for some reason they all maintained, that he was not dying, but was simply ill..." - Leo Tolstoy
  • "He wept on account of his helplessness, his terrible loneliness, the cruelty of man, the cruelty of God, the absence of God." - Leo Tolstoy
  • "He sought his former accustomed fear of death and did not find it." - Leo Tolstoy
