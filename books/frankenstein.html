Frankenstein by Mary Shelley

Published: 1818

Often called the first science fiction novel, Frankenstein is a tragic tale of ambition and abandonment. Victor Frankenstein creates a living being but is horrified by his creation and flees. The creature, left to navigate a hostile world alone, turns to violence. It's a profound look at what it means to be human and the responsibility of a creator.

Notable Quotes

  • "I ought to be thy Adam, but I am rather the fallen angel..." - Mary Shelley
  • "There is love in me the likes of which you've never seen. There is rage in me the likes of which should never escape. If I am not satisfied in the one, I will indulge the other." - Mary Shelley
  • "Beware; for I am fearless, and therefore powerful." - Mary Shelley
  • "Life, although it may only be an accumulation of anguish, is dear to me, and I will defend it." - Mary Shelley
  • "Nothing is so painful to the human mind as a great and sudden change." - Mary Shelley
  • "If I cannot inspire love, I will cause fear!" - Mary Shelley
  • "The world was to me a secret which I desired to divine." - Mary Shelley
