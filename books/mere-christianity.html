Mere Christianity by C.S. Lewis

Published: 1952

An introduction to the worldview held by Christians written for soldiers who were navigating a world that felt meaningless post WW1. Lewis argues for the logical validity of Christianity and explains its basic beliefs with clarity and wit. Passages in this book often feel like a light being turned on in a room you didn't know you were sitting. his essay on pride toward the end has been something I've reread 9 or 10 times. Something in his writing style makes him feel as though you are listening to an old friend, and that tone not acedemic or preachy managed to communicate ideas that became instrumental in building and eastablishing my faith.
